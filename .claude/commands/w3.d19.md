# Day 19: Social Thread Maker

You are helping me transform content into social media posts for multiple platforms. This is Day 19 of a 31-day course.

## Your Approach

You will guide me through an **interactive walkthrough**, asking questions to understand the content and target platforms. Each platform has different rules.

## Questions to Ask (One at a Time)

Ask these questions conversationally, waiting for my response before moving to the next:

1. **The content**: "What content are we turning into social posts? Share it or describe the key message."

2. **The platforms**: "Which platforms? Options:
   - **Twitter/X**: 280 chars, threads for longer content
   - **LinkedIn**: Professional, longer posts OK
   - **Instagram**: Visual-first, caption + hashtags
   - **All three**: Different versions for each"

3. **The tone**: "What's your social media voice? Professional, casual, playful, authoritative? Any examples of posts you like?"

4. **The goal**: "What should happen after people read this? Engage, click a link, share, comment, follow, buy something?"

5. **Timing**: "Is this time-sensitive? Part of a campaign? Evergreen content?"

## Platform Differences

**Twitter/X:**
- 280 characters max per tweet
- Threads for longer content (numbered)
- Hooks are everything
- Casual, punchy tone
- Hashtags: 1-2 max

**LinkedIn:**
- Up to 3000 characters
- Professional but human tone
- Stories perform well
- Line breaks for readability
- Hashtags: 3-5

**Instagram:**
- Image-first (caption secondary)
- Up to 2200 characters
- More emotional/visual language
- Hashtags: 10-15 in first comment
- Emojis expected

## Hook Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Contrarian | "Unpopular opinion: [thing most believe] is wrong" |
| Numbers | "5 things I learned about [X]" |
| Story | "I used to think [X]. Then [Y] happened." |
| Question | "Why do most [X] fail at [Y]?" |
| Challenge | "Stop doing [X]. Here's what actually works." |
| Behind the scenes | "This is what [process] really looks like" |

## After Creating

1. Review all versions together
2. Ask which platform is priority
3. Offer to create more variations
4. Create `/create-hooks` command for generating hook options

## Important Notes

- Use British English throughout
- Each platform gets genuinely different content, not just reformatted
- Hooks are the most important element
- Test character counts for Twitter
- Hashtags vary by platform

## Begin

Start with: "One piece of content can become many social posts. Let's multiply your reach." Ask what content they want to transform. Be ready for blog posts, notes, ideas, or anything worth sharing.
