### Step 3: Platform-Specific Best Practices (5 minutes)

**Say:**
"Each social platform has its own culture and constraints. Let's break down the big three:

**Twitter/X:**

- 280 characters per post
- Threads work well for storytelling
- Punchy, opinionated, conversational
- Use line breaks for readability
- First tweet is everything (the hook)

**LinkedIn:**

- 3,000 character limit (much more room)
- Professional tone, but increasingly casual
- First 2 lines determine if people click 'see more'
- Lists and bullet points perform well
- Comments drive algorithm favourability

**Threads:**

- 500 characters per post
- Conversational, authentic, less polished than Twitter
- Shorter threads (3-5 posts) perform better
- Text-first, images secondary

Same content, three different approaches. Let me show you."

**Action:**

### Step 4: Crafting the Perfect Hook (6 minutes)

**Say:**
"Right, let's talk about the most important part: the hook.

Your first line determines if someone reads the rest. On Twitter, it's the first tweet. On LinkedIn, it's the first two lines before 'see more'. On Threads, it's the opening of post 1.

Great hooks do one of these things:

1. **Challenge a belief** - 'Everyone says X, but here's why they're wrong...'
2. **Make a promise** - 'Here's how to [desirable outcome] in [timeframe]...'
3. **Tell a story** - 'Last Tuesday, something strange happened...'
4. **Ask a question** - 'Have you ever wondered why [intriguing thing]?'
5. **Share a surprising fact** - 'X% of people don't know this about [topic]...'

Let me generate five different hooks for your content."

## Common Questions & Answers

### Q: How long should a thread be?

**A:** Twitter: 5-10 tweets is ideal (attention drops after that). LinkedIn: Single post preferred, or 2-3 carousel posts. Threads: 3-5 posts work best. Longer threads can work if every post delivers value, but shorter usually performs better.

### Q: Should I use emojis?

**A:** Platform-dependent. Twitter: Yes, they add personality and visual breaks. LinkedIn: Sparingly, keep it professional. Threads: Yes, matches the casual vibe. Match your audience's expectations.

### Q: What about using AI-generated content on social media?

**A:** Be transparent if your platform/audience expects it. Use Claude to structure and refine your ideas, but add your personal voice, stories, and insights. Nobody wants generic AI content, but AI-assisted writing that reflects your genuine thinking is valuable.

### Q: How do I know if my hook is working?

**A:** Test multiple hooks with small audiences first. Track engagement rates (likes, replies, shares) in the first hour. The hook is working if people engage with the first post AND continue reading. High engagement on post 1 but drop-off after means the hook promised something the thread didn't deliver.

### Q: Can I reuse content across platforms?

**A:** Yes, but adapt it. Don't just copy-paste. Shift the tone, adjust the length, and respect each platform's culture. Think of it as translation, not duplication.

### Q: What's the best time to post?

**A:** Varies by platform and audience. Generally: LinkedIn weekday mornings (7-9am), Twitter early afternoon (12-3pm), Threads early evening (6-9pm). Test with your specific audience and adjust.
