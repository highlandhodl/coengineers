### Step 5: Common Creative Pitfalls (4 minutes)

**Say:**
"Before we wrap up, let's talk about common mistakes people make with creative outputs - and how to avoid them.

**Pitfall 1: Creating in isolation**
Don't make a presentation, then social posts, then a video with completely different messaging. Plan the unified message first, then express it across formats.

**Pitfall 2: Format before purpose**
Don't think 'we need a video' - think 'we need to communicate X, and video is the best format because Y'. Purpose drives format choice.

**Pitfall 3: Skipping the brief**
Don't jump straight to creating. Review the brief, understand constraints, identify the core message. Rushing this leads to content that misses the mark.

**Pitfall 4: One-and-done mentality**
Don't accept the first draft. Every lesson this week included iteration. Generate variations, test different approaches, refine.

**Pitfall 5: Forgetting the audience**
Don't create what you want - create what your audience needs. Technical diagrams for non-technical users? Won't work. Casual social threads for formal contexts? Misses the mark.

Which of these do you think you're most likely to fall into?"

## Common Questions & Answers

### Q: Which creative format should I master first?

**A:** Start with the format your work demands most. If you do presentations weekly, master that. If you manage social accounts, focus there. Depth in one beats surface knowledge of all six.

### Q: Can Claude actually create the final deliverables?

**A:** Not always. Claude creates scripts, outlines, prompts, and briefs - the instructions for creating. You (or your tools) execute: record the audio, generate the images, edit the video, render the diagrams.

### Q: How do I know if my creative output is good?

**A:** Test with your target audience. Show the presentation to a colleague. Share the social thread with friends. Get feedback before publishing. Good creative work resonates with its intended audience.

### Q: What if I'm not naturally creative?

**A:** Creative work is a skill, not a talent. Follow the framework: brief â†’ format â†’ structure â†’ iterate â†’ output. Claude handles the creative variations, you handle the strategic decisions.

### Q: Should I use templates for creative work?

**A:** Templates provide structure (good) but can limit creativity (bad). Use them as starting points, then adapt to your specific needs. This week taught you patterns, not rigid templates.
